About The Organization

Who are we?

WINTIMA is a nonprofit organisation dedicated to improving the quality of basic education in Ghana.

Owing to the fact that a good basic education is a foundation of excellence in academia as well as life, we strive to provide less privileged children with all the necessary materials required for school children at the basic level.

Our aim is to get as many children as possible to value and have a good basic education.

Additionally, we provide mentorship sessions for students to guide them through their education, from grade school to further studies. We provide guide for life during and after grade school.
Our beginning

WINTIMA was founded in December 2015 by Janet Zeylisa Dauda.

Together with a group of young and hardworking change makers, Janet, in 2015, reached out to about 200 school children in the Garu-Tempane district of the Upper East Region, Ghana with learning materials comprising text books, exercise books, story books, pencils and pens.

This was to be the start of WINTIMA as she yearned to create a positive impact in the lives of more rural school children who were deprived of some of these basic school necessities.
Since 2015

After its inception, WINTIMA has come into contact with and positively influenced the lives of about 600 children in the Garu-Tempane district.

In 2016, a mentorship session was held for close to 400 students from three Junior High Schools in the district.

Campaigns to halt early child marriage and advocate girl child education have been consistently held in the district.

Currently, WINTIMA is on course to clothing school children in a project dubbed “PUT A UNIFORM ON A CHILD”.

Making education appealing to the underprivileged young Ghanaian.
